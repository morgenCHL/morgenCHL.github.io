Basic Schema: Exercise-02

  • Argument
  • Academic Writing
Claim:
  • Practicing the piano is good for kids
Reason:
  • because it teaches discipline.
Grounds:
  • Examples showing that playing the piano enhance a kid’s discipline.
  • Cases or observation indicating that kids who plays piano behave decently and follow regulations.
  • Statistics revealing that kids who playing better piano behave more disciplined.
Warrant:
  • Kids should be taught discipline. An activity that teaches discipline is good.
Backing:
  • Arguments showing that disciplined kids have better personal development, have more significant achievement, and establish better rapport with others.
  • Examples of delinquent behavior or irresponsibility of undisciplined kids.
Rebutting the reason:
  • unless some who plays the piano but breaks the law.
  • unless those who play piano do not behave more disciplined (e.g., less moody, indecent, or careless).
  • unless kids can learn discipline through other approaches.
Rebutting the warrant:
  • unless kids should be raised naturally.
  • unless the discipline refrains kids from being themselves and thus become psychologically unhealthy.
  • unless an individual should learn discipline after adolescent.
Qualifier:
  • Practicing the piano may enhance a kid’s discipline.
  • Practicing the piano could be good for kids because they can learn discipline from it.
