Basic Schema: Exercise-03

  • Argument
  • Academic Writing
Claim:
  • Welfare benefits for unwed mothers should not be eliminated
Reason:
  • because these benefits are needed to prevent unbearable poverty among our nation’s most helpless citizens.
Grounds:
  • statistics reporting that unwed mothers are helpless citizens and suffer poverty.
  • examples showing that welfare benefits prevent unwed mothers from being poor.
  • interviews with unwed mothers indicating that these welfare benefits are necessary and helpful.
Warrant:
  • We should prevent poverty among our nation’s most helpless citizens.
Backing:
  • Arguments showing that it is humane to support those in need.
  • Arguments suggesting that eliminating poverty fosters a nation’s development.
  • Arguments warning that ignoring the need of most helpless citizens may result in a rise in crime.
Rebutting the reason:
  • unless these benefits are not enough to ameliorate poverty.
  • unless these benefits are not effectively given to the most helpless citizens (i.e., a corrupt bureaucracy).
  • unless poverty should be ameliorated by other approaches (e.g., increase job opportunities and provide employment counselling as well as career guidance).
Rebutting the warrant:
  • unless our nation is suffering a significant economic crisis and thus currently cannot afford to taking care of the most helpless citizens.
  • unless the nation’s religion supports Chauvinism or a Caste system.
  • unless the most critical challenges of the helpless residents is not poverty (e.g., human rights).
Qualifier:
  • Welfare benefits for unwed mothers should not be eliminated because it may help those helpless.
